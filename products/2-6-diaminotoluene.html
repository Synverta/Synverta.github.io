Overview

2,6-Diaminotoluene (2,6-Toluenediamine) is an aromatic compound bearing two amino groups in the 2 and 6 positions relative to a methyl substituent. It is commonly used as a building block for dyes and pigments, as an intermediate in polymer and resin manufacture, and in specialized chemical syntheses. Commercial material is available in technical grades for industrial use and higher-purity grades for research.

Common Synonyms

  • 2,6-Toluenediamine
  • Toluene-2,6-diamine
  • 2-Methyl-1,3-benzenediamine
  • 2,6-Tolylenediamine

Typical Applications

  • Intermediate for azo and direct dye manufacture
  • Precursor in polymer and resin chemistry (polyurethanes, polyamides)
  • Component in oxidative hair dye formulations (regulated use)
  • Specialty chemical synthesis and research reagent
  • Photoresist and electronic materials intermediates

Handling & Safety

2,6-Diaminotoluene is hazardous and should be handled by trained personnel using appropriate controls:

  • May cause skin and eye irritation; can be a sensitizer. Use gloves, eye protection and protective clothing.
  • Avoid inhalation of dust or vapors; use local exhaust or respiratory protection as needed.
  • Some aromatic diamines are associated with mutagenic and carcinogenic concerns—follow supplier SDS and local regulations for classification and use restrictions.
  • In case of contact, wash affected area with plenty of water and seek medical attention for serious exposures.
  • Prevent release to the environment and dispose of waste as hazardous chemical waste per local rules.

Packaging & Storage

  • Typical packaging: small bottles for lab use (25 g, 100 g), larger drums or bags for industrial quantities.
  • Store in a cool, dry, well-ventilated area; keep containers tightly closed and protected from moisture and strong oxidizers.
  • Recommended storage temperature: ambient to refrigerated conditions depending on supplier guidance.
