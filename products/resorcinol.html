Overview

Resorcinol (1,3-benzenediol) is a white crystalline phenolic compound widely used as a chemical intermediate and in industrial applications such as adhesives, resins, dyes, and pharmaceuticals. It provides good crosslinking activity in phenolic resins and contributes to UV-stable performance in formulations.

Common Synonyms

  • 1,3-Benzenediol
  • m-Dihydroxybenzene
  • 3-Hydroxyphenol
  • Resorcine

Typical Applications

  • Adhesives and resins (e.g., phenolic resins for wood adhesives)
  • Pharmaceutical and dermatological preparations
  • Hair dyes and cosmetics (component in formulations)
  • Dye and pigment intermediates, UV stabilizers
  • Corrosion inhibitors and specialty chemical synthesis

Handling & Safety

Resorcinol may be harmful if swallowed or absorbed through the skin and can cause severe eye irritation. It can cause methemoglobinemia at high exposures and may be hazardous to aquatic life. Follow safety measures:

  • Wear appropriate PPE: gloves, safety goggles, lab coat, and respiratory protection if dust/vapors may be generated.
  • Avoid inhalation of dust and prolonged skin contact.
  • In case of skin or eye contact flush thoroughly with water and seek medical attention.
  • Consult SDS for full GHS hazard statements, exposure limits, and first-aid measures.

Packaging & Storage

  • Typical packaging: 25 kg polyethylene-lined fiber drums; small pack sizes (100 g, 250 g, 500 g) for laboratory use.
  • Storage conditions: store in a cool, dry, well-ventilated area away from oxidizing agents and moisture; keep container tightly closed.
  • Shelf life: per supplier guidance — store protected from light and moisture to avoid discoloration.
