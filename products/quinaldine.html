Quinaldine (2-Methylquinoline)

An aromatic heterocycle used as a building block for dyes, pharmaceuticals and analytical reagents; often supplied as a colorless to yellow liquid.

Back to products

Overview

Quinaldine (2-methylquinoline) is a methyl-substituted quinoline used widely as an intermediate in dye chemistry, pharmaceuticals, and as a research reagent. It can be handled as a liquid; samples may darken on exposure to air and light.

Common Synonyms

  • 2-Methylquinoline
  • 2-methyl-quinoline
  • Methylquinoline
  • Quinaldine

Typical Applications

  • Intermediate for dye and pigment manufacture
  • Pharmaceutical intermediate
  • Analytical reagent and chemical sensor synthesis
  • Fish anaesthetic in aquaculture (restricted use)

Typical Specifications

  • Purity: commercial grades from 95% to ≥98% (GC)
  • Appearance: colorless to pale yellow liquid; color limits depend on batch
  • Packaging: amber glass bottles for small volumes; larger containers for bulk

Handling & Safety

Quinaldine is harmful and can cause skin and eye irritation; avoid inhalation and environmental release:

  • Use appropriate PPE: gloves, goggles and protective clothing.
  • Avoid inhalation of vapors; use in well-ventilated area or fume hood.
  • In case of exposure, rinse affected areas and seek medical attention.
  • Consult SDS for full hazard statements, exposure limits and first-aid instructions.

Packaging & Storage

  • Packaging: glass bottles (25 mL–1 L) for lab use, drums for bulk shipping.
  • Storage: store in a cool, ventilated, dark place; protect from moisture and light.
  • Transport: regulated based on hazard classification—follow supplier guidance.
