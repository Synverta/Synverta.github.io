Isophorone diisocyanate (IPDI)

An aliphatic cycloaliphatic diisocyanate used to produce UV- and weather-resistant polyurethanes for coatings, elastomers, adhesives and specialty polymers.

Back to products

Overview

Isophorone diisocyanate (IPDI) is a cycloaliphatic diisocyanate notable for producing polyurethanes with excellent UV stability, weather resistance and mechanical performance. It is commonly used where light stability and durability are required.

Common Synonyms

  • 3-Isocyanatomethyl-3,5,5-trimethylcyclohexyl isocyanate
  • 5-Isocyanato-1-(isocyanatomethyl)-1,3,3-trimethylcyclohexane
  • IPDI

Typical Applications

  • Aliphatic polyurethane coatings (automotive, aerospace, industrial)
  • Elastomers and sealants requiring UV resistance
  • Adhesives and binders for specialty polymers
  • Flexible and rigid polyurethane systems in specialty applications

  • Color: ≤ 30 Hazen (APHA), product/grade dependent
  • Hydrolysable chlorine and trace impurities controlled per supplier specs

Handling & Safety

IPDI is hazardous and moisture sensitive. It can cause severe irritation and sensitization; inhalation may be fatal. Follow these precautions:

  • Use in well-ventilated areas or with local exhaust; avoid inhalation of vapors and aerosols.
  • Wear suitable PPE: chemical-resistant gloves, eye/face protection, protective clothing; use appropriate respiratory protection if exposures may exceed limits.
  • Avoid contact with water, alcohols, amines and acids (reactive with water and protic materials).
  • In case of skin contact, wash thoroughly and seek medical attention for severe exposures; for inhalation remove to fresh air and seek immediate medical care.
  • Consult supplier SDS for detailed emergency and first-aid procedures, exposure limits and regulatory classification.

Packaging & Storage

  • Typical packaging: glass or metal bottles and drums; many shipments are under dry inert gas (nitrogen).
  • Storage: keep containers tightly closed in a cool, dry, well-ventilated place; protect from moisture and store refrigerated (0–10 °C) when recommended by supplier.
  • Shelf life: follows supplier guidance (often ~12 months unopened under recommended storage).
  • Transport: follow local regulations; package to prevent moisture ingress and protect from heat.
