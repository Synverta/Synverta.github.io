Sodium (Na)

A soft, silvery-white, highly reactive alkali metal used as a reducing agent, in metal refining, heat-transfer applications and in specialized chemical syntheses.

Back to products

Overview

Sodium (CAS 7440-23-5) is a highly reactive alkali metal, commonly stored under oil or an inert atmosphere to prevent reaction with air and moisture. It is widely used as a laboratory and industrial reagent and has niche roles in metal processing and nuclear engineering.

Common Names

  • Sodium
  • Natrium
  • Sodium metal

Typical Applications

  • Reducing agent in chemical synthesis and organic reductions
  • Manufacture of sodium compounds (hydride, peroxide, alcoholates)
  • Coolant in fast-breeder nuclear reactors
  • Metal refining and alloy production
  • Sodium vapor lamps and specialty electrical applications

Typical Specifications

  • Purity: commonly 99–99.95% for reagent/metallic grades
  • Form: ingots, sticks, rods, shot
  • Surface protection: supplied under mineral/paraffin oil or inert gas
  • Typical impurity limits: Fe ≤0.001%, Cl ≤0.002% (supplier-dependent)

Handling & Safety

Sodium is extremely reactive and poses significant fire and explosion risks on contact with water. Key precautions include:

  • Store under inert atmosphere or hydrocarbon oil; keep away from moisture and acids.
  • Use non-sparking tools and avoid contact with water; in case of fire use Class D dry powder extinguishers.
  • Wear full PPE: face shield, goggles, chemical-resistant gloves and flame-resistant clothing when handling larger quantities.
  • For spills, cover with dry sand or use an appropriate Class D extinguisher—do not use water.
  • Review supplier SDS for detailed emergency and disposal procedures.

Packaging & Storage

  • Packaging: metal cans, drums or sealed ampoules under oil or inert gas; small ampoules for lab use.
  • Storage: water-free, cool environment in sealed containers under oil or inert gas; segregate from oxidizers and acids.
  • Transport: regulated as hazardous material (UN 1428); follow regional transport regulations.
