2-Methyl-4-nitroaniline

An aromatic nitroaniline used as an intermediate in dye and agrochemical synthesis; handled as a solid intermediate with specific safety controls due to nitro/aromatic functionality.

Back to products

Overview

2-Methyl-4-nitroaniline is a substituted aniline bearing a nitro group. It serves as a precursor for dyes, pigments, and specialty chemicals. It is typically supplied as a crystalline solid[...]

Synonyms

  • 2-methyl-4-nitroaniline
  • 4-nitro-2-methylaniline

Physical & Chemical Properties

PropertyTypical Value
Chemical formulaC7H8N2O2
Molecular weight152.15 g·mol⁻¹
AppearanceYellow to orange crystalline powder
Melting point~110–116 °C
SolubilitySparingly soluble in water; soluble in organic solvents like ethanol, acetone, dichloromethane

Handling & Safety

Nitroanilines present nitro and aniline hazards — toxic if absorbed and potentially harmful to aquatic life. Observe protective measures.
