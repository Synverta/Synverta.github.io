2-Methyl-4-nitroaniline

An aromatic nitroaniline used as an intermediate in dye and agrochemical synthesis; handled as a solid intermediate with specific safety controls due to nitro/aromatic amine hazards.

Overview

2-Methyl-4-nitroaniline is a substituted aniline bearing a nitro group. It serves as a precursor for dyes, pigments, and specialty chemicals. It is typically supplied as a crystalline solid and used in controlled industrial processes.

Synonyms

  • 2-methyl-4-nitroaniline
  • 4-nitro-2-methylaniline

Applications

  • Intermediate for dyes, azo coupling and pigments
  • Agrochemical intermediates and specialty organic synthesis
  • Research reagent in synthetic chemistry

Physical & Chemical Properties

SolubilitySparingly soluble in water; soluble in organic solvents like ethanol, acetone, dichloromethane

Specifications

  • Purity: typically ≥95–99% (supplier dependent)
  • Appearance: crystalline powder; color may vary

Handling & Safety

Nitroanilines present both nitro and aniline hazards — toxic if absorbed and potentially harmful to aquatic life. Observe:

  • Use gloves, eye protection and dust control when handling the powder.
  • Avoid inhalation; work in ventilation or fume hood.
  • In case of exposure, follow standard first-aid and seek medical attention for severe effects.

Packaging & Storage

  • Packaging: sealed bottles, bags or drums depending on quantity.
  • Storage: store in a cool, dry, ventilated area away from reducing agents and strong bases; keep container tightly closed.
