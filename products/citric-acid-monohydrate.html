Overview

Citric acid monohydrate is the hydrated crystalline form of citric acid commonly used in food, pharmaceutical, cosmetic and industrial applications as an acidulant, chelating agent, and d[...]

Common Synonyms

  • Citric acid monohydrate
  • 2-Hydroxypropane-1,2,3-tricarboxylic acid monohydrate
  • Citric acid hydrate
  • Acidum citricum monohydricum

Typical Applications

  • Food and beverage acidulant (E330), flavoring and preservative
  • Pharmaceutical excipient and buffer component
  • Detergents and descalers for household and industrial cleaning
  • Cosmetics and personal care formulations as pH adjuster and chelating agent
  • Laboratory reagent and analytical chemistry standard

Typical Specifications

  • Purity: ≥98%–99.7% depending on grade (food, pharma, reagent)
  • Water content: typically ~7.5–8.8% for monohydrate
  • Heavy metals: controlled per grade (e.g., ≤5 mg/kg)
  • Conforms to USP/EP/food-grade specifications when specified

Handling & Safety

Citric acid monohydrate is an irritant to eyes and respiratory tract. Use protective measures:

  • Avoid inhalation of dust; use local exhaust ventilation.
  • Wear protective eyewear and gloves to prevent eye and skin contact.
  • In case of eye contact, rinse thoroughly with water and seek medical attention if irritation persists.
  • Store in a cool, dry place away from strong oxidizers and bases.

Packaging & Storage

  • Packaging: sealed PE bags, drums, HDPE containers, and laboratory vials.
  • Storage: keep tightly closed in a dry, cool, and well-ventilated area; avoid moisture to prevent caking.
  • Shelf life: stable when stored properly.
