Isophoronediamine (IPDA)

Cycloaliphatic diamine used primarily as an epoxy curing agent and chemical intermediate; low color, good chemical and mechanical performance for high-performance coatings, adhesives and composites.

Back to products

Overview

Isophoronediamine (commonly abbreviated IPDA) is a cycloaliphatic diamine supplied as a mixture of stereoisomers. It is widely used as a polyamine curing agent for epoxy resins and as an intermediate in specialty chemical synthesis. IPDA offers low yellowing, good reactivity and results in cured materials with excellent mechanical and chemical resistance.

Common Synonyms

  • Isophoronediamine (IPDA)
  • 1-Amino-3-aminomethyl-3,5,5-trimethylcyclohexane
  • 3-Aminomethyl-3,5,5-trimethylcyclohexylamine
  • Isophorondiamine

Typical Applications

  • Epoxy curing agent for industrial and flooring coatings, adhesives, and composite systems
  • Intermediate for polyurethanes, diisocyanates and specialty amine derivatives
  • Formulations requiring improved chemical and heat resistance and low color development
  • Specialty chemical syntheses and corrosion inhibitors

Handling & Safety

IPDA is a corrosive and sensitizing amine. It may cause severe skin burns and eye damage and can cause allergic skin reactions on sensitized individuals. Observe the following:

  • Use appropriate PPE: chemical-resistant gloves, face/eye protection and protective clothing.
  • Avoid inhalation of vapors or mists — work in well-ventilated areas or use local exhaust.
  • In case of skin contact: remove contaminated clothing and wash thoroughly with soap and water; seek medical attention for severe exposures.
  • Store in tightly closed containers in a cool, dry, well-ventilated place away from oxidizers and acids.
  • Refer to supplier Safety Data Sheet (SDS) for full GHS hazard statements and first-aid measures.

Packaging & Storage

  • Common packaging: 200 kg epoxy-lined steel drums for bulk; small volumes in sealed bottles for laboratory use.
  • Storage conditions: store below 30 °C, protect from moisture and air exposure; keep containers tightly closed.
  • Transport: assigned UN number and packaging based on local regulations — consult SDS (UN 2735 typically used for amines/corrosives; classification may vary by supplier/region).
