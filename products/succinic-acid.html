Succinic acid

A biobased dicarboxylic acid (butanedioic acid) used as a building block for polymers, food acidulant, pharmaceutical intermediate and industrial chemical intermediate.

Back to products

Overview

Succinic acid (butanedioic acid) is a four-carbon dicarboxylic acid occurring in metabolism and widely used across chemical, food and pharmaceutical industries. It is a precursor to biodegradable polymers, plasticizers, resins, and serves as an acidulant in food products.

Common Synonyms

  • Butanedioic acid
  • Amber acid
  • 1,2-Ethanedicarboxylic acid
  • Succinate (salt form)

Typical Applications

  • Precursor for polybutylene succinate and other biodegradable polymers
  • Food acidulant and flavoring agent
  • Pharmaceutical intermediates and excipients
  • Cosmetics and personal care pH adjuster
  • Chemical intermediate for dyes, coatings, and adhesives

Typical Specifications

  • Purity: ≥99.0% (typical commercial grades)
  • Moisture: ≤0.5%
  • Heavy metals: controlled per specification
  • Fumaric acid and other isomeric impurities controlled per supplier

Handling & Safety

Succinic acid is generally of low hazard but can cause irritation to skin and eyes and respiratory tract in dust form. Use protective measures:

  • Wear gloves, eye protection and dust mask when handling powder.
  • Avoid inhalation of dust; use ventilation or respiratory protection if needed.
  • Store dry and manage spills as non-flammable solid waste.

Packaging & Storage

  • Packaging: bottles, drums, bulk bags, ISO tanks for large volumes.
  • Storage: cool, dry, well-ventilated area away from strong oxidizers and bases.
  • Shelf life: stable for years if stored sealed and dry.
