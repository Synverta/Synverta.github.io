DL-Malic acid

The racemic form of malic acid (DL‑malic acid), a dicarboxylic acid used as an acidulant, flavor modifier and chemical intermediate in food, cosmetic and specialty chemical applications.

Back to products

Overview

DL‑Malic acid is the racemic mixture of the naturally occurring L‑malic acid and its D‑enantiomer. It is a white crystalline powder with pronounced tartness and is widely used as an acidulant and flavoring agent in foods and beverages. It is also used in cosmetics, pharmaceuticals and as a reagent in chemical synthesis.

Common Synonyms

  • DL-Malic acid
  • (±)-Malic acid
  • DL-Hydroxysuccinic acid
  • Hydroxybutanedioic acid
  • E296 (food additive code)

Typical Applications

  • Acidulant and flavor enhancer in beverages, confectionery and processed foods
  • pH adjuster in cosmetics and personal care formulations
  • Pharmaceutical excipient and intermediate for organic synthesis
  • Ingredient in chelating and cleaning formulations
  • Research reagent for laboratory use

Handling & Safety

DL‑Malic acid is generally low‑toxicity but can cause eye irritation and skin irritation in concentrated form. Follow standard chemical hygiene practices:

  • Use gloves, eye protection and protective clothing to avoid dust contact.
  • Avoid inhalation of dust; use local exhaust or respiratory protection if dust may be generated.
  • In case of eye contact, rinse thoroughly with water and seek medical attention if irritation persists.
  • Refer to supplier SDS for specific first aid, storage and disposal guidance.

Packaging & Storage

  • Common packaging: sealed polyethylene bags (food-grade) in cartons, HDPE drums, and smaller laboratory bottle sizes (100 g, 500 g, 1 kg).
  • Storage: store in a cool, dry, well‑ventilated area away from moisture and strong oxidizers; keep containers tightly closed.
  • Shelf life: follow supplier guidance; stable if stored as recommended.
