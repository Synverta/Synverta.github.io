Melamine (1,3,5-Triazine-2,4,6-triamine)

A triazine compound used principally as a precursor to melamine resins, laminates, adhesives and flame-retardant materials.

Overview

Melamine is a nitrogen-rich heterocyclic compound notable for its high nitrogen content and ability to form strong thermoset resins with formaldehyde. Melamine-formaldehyde resins are used in laminates, coatings, molding compounds and adhesives.

Common Synonyms

  • Melamine
  • 1,3,5-Triazine-2,4,6-triamine
  • Triazine triamine

Typical Applications

  • Melamine-formaldehyde resins for laminates and molding compounds
  • Flame-retardant additives and coatings
  • Adhesives, paper impregnation and decorative surfaces
  • Industrial intermediate in polymer chemistry

Typical Specifications

  • Purity: typically ≥99% for industrial/resin grades
  • Appearance: white crystalline powder
  • Moisture and ash controlled per supplier COA

Handling & Safety

Melamine is low in acute toxicity but can cause irritation to eyes and respiratory tract if dust is inhaled. Follow standard precautions:

  • Use dust control, gloves, and eye protection in handling powdered material.
  • Avoid ingestion and prolonged skin contact.
  • Store in a dry place away from strong oxidizing agents and formaldehyde sources until intended for resin production.

Packaging & Storage

  • Packaging: lined bags, drums, bulk bags (ton bags) for industrial volumes.
  • Storage: cool, dry, well-ventilated area; protect from moisture.
