Ascorbic acid (Vitamin C)

A water-soluble essential vitamin and antioxidant used in pharmaceuticals, food fortification, cosmetics and as a laboratory reagent.

Overview

Ascorbic acid (Vitamin C) is widely used as a dietary supplement, antioxidant preservative and reagent in chemical and biochemical laboratories. It participates in redox reactions and biological processes such as collagen biosynthesis and immune function. Commercial grades cover pharmaceutical, food and reagent specifications.

Common Synonyms

  • L-Ascorbic acid
  • Vitamin C
  • Antiscorbutic factor
  • Ascorbate

Typical Applications

  • Food and beverage fortification and preservation
  • Pharmaceutical formulations (tablets, injectables, topical)
  • Cosmetic antioxidant and preservative
  • Laboratory reagent for redox chemistry and analytical assays
  • Industrial additive in metal treatment and polymer stabilization

  • Heavy metals: controlled to low ppm (supplier-specific)

Handling & Safety

Ascorbic acid is generally low-hazard but dusty material can irritate eyes, skin and respiratory tract. Follow standard precautions:

  • Use appropriate PPE (gloves, eye protection, dust control) during handling.
  • Avoid generation of dust; use local exhaust ventilation if necessary.
  • Store in a cool, dry, dark place; avoid strong oxidizers and metals that catalyze degradation.
  • Refer to supplier SDS for emergency measures and specific incompatibilities.

Packaging & Storage

  • Common packaging: sealed plastic or glass containers (25 g — 5 kg), drums for bulk shipments.
  • Storage: cool, dry, dark conditions (refrigeration for long-term storage recommended by some suppliers).
  • Shelf life: varies by grade and storage; follow supplier COA/SDS for expiry.
